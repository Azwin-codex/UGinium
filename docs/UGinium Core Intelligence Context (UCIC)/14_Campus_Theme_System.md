# CHAPTER 14

# Campus Theme System Specification

---

## Purpose

The Campus Theme System allows every university to preserve its own identity while remaining part of the UGinium ecosystem.

Each university should immediately feel familiar to its students through its institutional branding, yet unmistakably remain UGinium in architecture, interaction, and user experience.

Campus themes customize the visual identity.

They never redefine the product.

---

# 14.1 Philosophy

UGinium is one platform.

Universities are individual Campuses.

Every Campus shares the same architecture, interaction language, and user experience.

Only the visual identity adapts.

Students should instantly recognize:

* Their university.
* UGinium.

Both identities should coexist without conflict.

---

# 14.2 Campus Identity

Every university receives its own Campus configuration.

A Campus contains:

* University Name
* Official Logo
* Official Colors
* Campus Banner
* University Metadata
* Academic Departments
* Student Communities
* Official Events

Campus identity personalizes the experience while preserving platform consistency.

---

# 14.3 Customizable Elements

Each Campus may customize:

Primary Accent Color

Secondary Accent Color

Orb Accent

Campus Banner

University Logo

Loading Illustration

Achievement Certificate Branding

Official Email Domain

Faculty Branding

Department Icons (optional)

These elements help students feel connected to their institution.

---

# 14.4 Protected Elements

The following elements must remain identical across every Campus:

Component Architecture

Module Design

Typography

Grid System

Spacing

Motion System

Navigation

Interaction Patterns

Information Hierarchy

Accessibility Standards

Progression System

Recognition System

The Academy always remains structurally consistent.

---

# 14.5 Theme Inheritance

Campus themes inherit from the Academy Theme.

```text
Academy Theme
        ↓
Campus Theme
        ↓
Department Theme (Future)
        ↓
Event Theme (Temporary)
```

Inheritance ensures consistency while allowing controlled customization.

---

# 14.6 Campus Color Rules

Campus colors are used only for:

Buttons

Interactive Highlights

Selected States

Progress Indicators

Orb Energy

Charts

Campus Branding

Loading Accents

They should never replace Academy surface colors.

The interface should remain predominantly composed of Academy materials.

---

# 14.7 University Branding

Every Campus may display:

Official Logo

University Name

Campus Motto (optional)

Verified Status

Academic Year

Official Events

Branding should remain elegant and unobtrusive.

UGinium remains the primary product identity.

---

# 14.8 Multi-Campus Consistency

Students transferring between universities should immediately understand the interface.

Changing Campus should feel like changing environments within the same operating system.

Architecture must remain identical.

Only identity changes.

---

# 14.9 Campus Administration

Each Campus should support administrative customization including:

Official Announcements

Campus Events

Hackathons

Research Opportunities

Faculty Notices

Placement Drives

Student Organizations

These should integrate naturally into existing Academy Modules.

---

# 14.10 Department Support (Future)

Campuses may later introduce department-level identities.

Examples:

Computer Science

Artificial Intelligence

Mechanical Engineering

Business

Design

Electronics

Departments inherit the Campus Theme.

They never replace it.

---

# 14.11 Seasonal Themes

Temporary themes may exist for:

Orientation

Hackathons

Graduation

Annual Tech Fest

Innovation Week

Research Expo

Seasonal themes may introduce temporary visual accents while preserving the Academy architecture.

---

# 14.12 Certificates & Documents

All official documents should include:

UGinium Identity

Campus Identity

Verification Information

Digital Authentication

Issue Date

Unique Certificate ID

Documents should feel premium and institutionally credible.

---

# 14.13 Theme Architecture

Campus customization should be entirely token-driven.

Every customizable property should exist as a design token.

No component should contain university-specific values.

Campus switching should require no UI redesign.

Only token replacement.

---

# 14.14 Developer Rules

Campus themes should be configurable through centralized theme files.

No hardcoded university assets.

Every Campus should inherit the Academy Design System automatically.

New universities should require configuration—not redesign.

---

# 14.15 Claude Rules

When designing Campus-specific interfaces:

Preserve the Academy architecture.

Apply Campus colors sparingly.

Respect official university branding.

Never redesign components for individual universities.

Ensure every Campus remains recognizably UGinium.

---

# 14.16 Review Checklist

Before implementation verify:

□ Does the interface still feel like UGinium?

□ Is university branding represented correctly?

□ Are Campus colors used appropriately?

□ Does architecture remain unchanged?

□ Is accessibility preserved?

□ Can additional universities be added easily?

□ Is every customization token-driven?

---

# 14.17 Non-Negotiable Rules

The Academy is universal.

Campuses are personalized.

Architecture never changes.

Only identity changes.

Universities personalize the experience.

UGinium defines the experience.

Every Campus belongs to the same Academy.
